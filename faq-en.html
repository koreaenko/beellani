Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q. Does semi-permanent makeup hurt a lot?

While individual pain tolerance varies, most clients describe it as a manageable level of stimulation. Pain intensity can differ based on skin condition and treatment area. We provide thorough guidance before the procedure.

Q. How long does it last?

Duration varies depending on technique, skin type, lifestyle, and aftercare, but typically fades gradually over about 1 year. Men tend to have shorter retention than women, and oily skin retains less than dry skin.

Q. Will the color change or turn red?

Color changes can depend on pigment used, skin type, and treatment depth. At Bellani, we select pigments considering skin tone and the passage of time, ensuring natural-looking results even as the color fades over time.

Q. Won't it look too dark at first?

Depending on the technique, the color may appear darker immediately after treatment. However, after the skin heals and peels within about a week, the color settles into a soft, natural shade.

Q. Can I get treatment if I have old pigment/traces?

Treatment is possible depending on the intensity and design of existing traces. Darker traces are typically treated with gradient technique. If you prefer a different technique, a removal treatment may be needed first.

Q. Is a touch-up mandatory?

Touch-ups are not mandatory and do not extend the retention period. They are corrective treatments to refine the initial work, and are recommended based on skin absorption rate and recovery status.

Q. When can I wash my face or wear makeup?

Please avoid washing the treated area on the day of treatment. Makeup can be applied the next day, avoiding the treatment area.

Q. Can pregnant women or sensitive skin types get treated?

Treatment is not available during pregnancy or breastfeeding. Those with sensitive skin may experience more pain or slower recovery.

Q. Can I return to daily activities right after?

Daily activities are generally possible, but swelling or redness may occur depending on the individual. The treated area may look unnatural during the peeling period (about one week), so please plan accordingly.

Q. Can results be corrected if I'm not satisfied?

We go through thorough consultation and design process before treatment. After the procedure, adjustments and refinements can be made through the touch-up process.
